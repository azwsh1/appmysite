## How AppMySite Actually Works

Let's walk through the real process of creating an app, step by step.

**Step 1: Sign Up and Choose Your App Type**

You create an account and pick which type of app you want to build. Options include Website to App (works with any website), WordPress to App, WooCommerce to App, Shopify to App, or Custom App (building from scratch without a website).

**Step 2: Connect Your Website**

If you're converting an existing site, you connect it to AppMySite. For WordPress, you install a plugin that handles the connection automatically. For other platforms, you provide your website URL and configure some basic settings.

**Step 3: Customize Your App Design**

This is where you make the app look like yours. Choose colors, upload your logo, design the app icon, configure the splash screen, set up navigation menus, and decide which pages appear in the app. The platform provides previews so you see changes in real-time.

**Step 4: Configure Features**

Enable or disable features like push notifications, social login, payment gateways, search functionality, and user accounts. Depending on your plan, you'll have different options available.

**Step 5: Test Your App**

Before publishing, test everything. AppMySite provides simulators for both iOS and Android so you can interact with your app before it goes live. You can also test on real devices using their demo app.

**Step 6: Prepare for Publishing**

Create developer accounts with Google Play and the Apple App Store (these cost $25 one-time for Google, $99/year for Apple). Generate app builds from AppMySite and download the files needed for submission.

**Step 7: Submit to App Stores**

Upload your app to Google Play Console and Apple App Connect. Follow their guidelines, fill out metadata, provide screenshots, and wait for review approval. This usually takes a few days to a couple weeks depending on the platform.

**Step 8: Manage and Update**

Once live, manage your app through the AppMySite dashboard. Content updates happen automatically when you update your website. For design changes or new features, you can push updates through the platform.

**Updates aren't automatic for major changes**. While content syncs automatically, if you make significant design changes or add new features, you might need to generate new app builds and update through the app stores.

**Consider the long game**. Monthly costs add up over time. Calculate total cost of ownership over a year or two to see if a lifetime plan might save money.
